# How to Use the MOSI-6 Framework Step-by-Step

Hormozi teaches that you should move down this list in order. If you have a problem at Step 1, solving Step 4 won't help you grow.

## Quick Reference

After confirming you can't just do **More** of what's already working, walk these constraints in order:

1. **Metrics:** Do you actually have the data? (You can't fix what you don't measure).
2. **Market:** Is there enough demand? Are you fishing in a pond with no fish?
3. **Model:** Is the math of the business broken? (e.g., it costs $100 to get a customer who only pays you $80).
4. **Money:** This refers to Cash Flow.
   - **Leads ↑:** Increasing the volume of potential interest.
   - **Sales ↑:** Improving the conversion of that interest.
   - **LTGP ↓:** (Lower Left) Likely referring to Long Term Gross Profit or Lifetime Value constraints.
   - **CF ↓:** Cash Flow issues (often caused by the "Success Gap" where you grow so fast you run out of cash).
5. **Manpower:** Do you have the people to fulfill the work?

## Step 1: MORE? (Input Volume)

Before changing anything, ask: "Can I just do more of what is already working?"

- **The Logic:** If you're making $10k/month spending 1 hour a day on cold calls, don't buy a new software. Spend 5 hours on cold calls.
- **Example:** A gym owner told Alex they weren't growing. Alex asked how many leads they got. The owner said "5 a week." Alex asked "How many could you handle?" The owner said "50." The solution wasn't a new "Model"; it was just doing 10x More of the current lead generation.

## Step 2: METRICS (The Dashboard)

If doing "More" didn't work, do you actually know why?

- **The Logic:** You cannot manage what you do not measure. You need to know your CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer), LTV (Lifetime Value), and Churn.
- **Example:** A software company thinks they have a lead problem because revenue is flat. Once they track Metrics, they realize they have plenty of leads, but their Close Rate is only 5%. The problem isn't marketing; it's sales.

## Step 3: MARKET (The Audience)

Is the "pond" you are fishing in big enough or interested enough?

- **The Logic:** You can have the best sales team, but if you are selling VHS tapes in 2024, you will fail.
- **Example:** Hormozi often cites Chiropractors. If a chiropractor only serves a 5-mile radius in a tiny town of 2,000 people, their "Market" is the constraint. No amount of "More" will fix a lack of humans.

## Step 4: MODEL (The Math)

Does the business actually make sense on paper?

- **The Logic:** If it costs you $1,000 to get a customer but they only pay you $800 over their lifetime, the more you grow, the faster you go broke.
- **Example:** Many agencies "scale" by hiring expensive account managers for every 5 clients. If the labor cost exceeds the retainer, the Model is broken. You need to change the pricing or the fulfillment method.

## Step 5: MONEY (Cash Flow & Economics)

This is where the sub-bullets on the whiteboard come in: Leads ↑, Sales ↑, LTGP ↓, CF ↓.

- **The Logic:** This is about the "Efficiency of Capital."
- **Leads/Sales:** Are you converting cash into customers efficiently?
- **LTGP (Long Term Gross Profit):** Are you making enough profit per customer to reinvest?
- **CF (Cash Flow):** Do you get paid before or after you do the work?
- **Example:** A construction company has $1M in sales but is "broke" because they pay for materials today and get paid by the client in 90 days. They have a Money/Cash Flow constraint.

## Step 6: MANPOWER (The Team)

This is the most important part of the framework.

- **The Logic:** Most people try to hire their way out of problems. Hormozi argues that you should only hire when your Model is so successful that you physically cannot handle the volume.
- **Example:** If you hire a Sales Manager (Manpower) before you have a proven Sales Script (Model) and Lead Flow (Money), that manager will sit around and do nothing. You must solve 1 through 5 before adding 6.
